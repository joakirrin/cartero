Goal:
Improve commit-summary quality and validate readiness for canonical-first migration.

User problem:
Commit summaries could be generic or too technical, especially without context, making it hard to understand the real problem and impact.

Decisions:
- implemented semantic validation and normalization for reason/impact
- added ambiguity-aware fallback for low-signal diffs
- introduced structured commit_fields and quality_metadata
- migrated CLI/web to structured-first consumption
- implemented readiness harness with curated corpus and metrics

Tradeoffs:
- kept YAML bridge for compatibility instead of removing it early
- limited readiness validation to a small corpus for now

User impact:
Developers get clearer, more reliable commit summaries, with better handling of ambiguous changes and improved confidence in output quality.
